About Me

Hi there! My name is Kyle, and I am an innovative developer and business student. My drive has left me with a wide portfolio of both technical, and non-technical projects, a 93% cumulative GPA, and extensive leadership experience through involving myself in things like case competitions, hackathons, and school clubs. When I am not glued to my desk staring at my computer screens, I enjoy spending time with friends, hiking, and exercising.

Try, fail, learn. The best way to learn is to push ourselves outside our comfort zone, try new things, gain new perspectives and to embrace failure. Through consistently adopting this mindset, I actively search for and am eager to take on new challenges. Whether it be taking ice cold showers for a month, conquering my fear of public speaking by joining Toastmasters, or simply taking on a project I have zero experience in, I never lose sight of what’s important – hopping in with both feet, loving the journey, and not giving up until I have achieved what I set out to.

Living this way has left me with a diverse set of experiences, both in and out of school. At school, some of these experiences include tutoring students who need help, studying business abroad in Asia, attending both business and computer science events, and staying active in clubs I enjoy. Outside of the classroom, I have been fortunate enough to compete in multiple business case competitions and hackathons, enroll in various online courses unaffiliated with UBC, and extensively explore BC’s mountain ranges.

In this era of drastic change, my adaptability, can-do attitude, and resilience have helped me to thrive in unfamiliar situations and persevere through challenges. I am a work-in-progress, and this drives me to move forward every day.

If you ever need a hiking buddy, a team member for a project or competition, or just want to connect to bounce around ideas or chat, do not hesitate to let me know! I am always looking for opportunities to connect with like-minded individuals.
